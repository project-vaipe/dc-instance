About Vietnam Data Commons

Vietnam Data Commons, powered by Data Commons, is a joint effort between VinUni-Illinois Smart Health Center at VinUniversity and The International Research Center for Artificial Intelligence (BKAI) at Hanoi University of Science and Technology (HUST) to provide an open web-based platform to highlight Vietnam-specific data across various domains. It features datasets published by Vietnamese Ministries and governmental organizations and have been well validated before published. We aim to provide a comprehensive view of the country's social, economic, education and environmental trends through our free dashboards and visualization tools. Data Commons synthesizes a single graph from different data sources. It links references to the same entities (such as cities, districts, states, etc.) across different datasets to nodes on the graph, so that users can access data about a particular entity aggregated from different sources without data cleaning or joining. We hope the data contained within Data Commons will be useful to students, researchers, and enthusiasts across different disciplines.

Hence, Vietnam Data Commons can be accessed by anyone (consumers, journalists, policymakers and researchers) via the tools available on our official website http://datacommons.vn/. Students, researchers and developers can use the provided APIs, all of which are free for educational, academic and journalistic research purposes.

Contributing to Vietnam Data Commons

Vietnam Data Commons is entirely open-source. Any Vietnamese dataset added to our platform are very welcome and will be featured in our official webpage. You can contribute to our database through our GitHub repo. More information on contributing can be found in our documentation. We are always looking for more collaborators, both for adding new data to Data Commons and for building new and interesting applications of Vietnam Data Commons. Contact us if you are interested in working with us. Mail us for any queries or suggestions. For more information on the Data Commons project, please visit this page.
